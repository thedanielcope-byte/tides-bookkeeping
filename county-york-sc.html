About the Area

York County Is SC's
Charlotte Overflow &
Fastest-Growing Market.

York County is the fourth most populous county in South Carolina and one of the fastest-growing counties in the entire United States. Sitting directly south of Charlotte, NC along the I-77 corridor, York County has become the premier destination for Charlotte-area families, remote workers, and businesses seeking South Carolina's lower taxes and cost of living without sacrificing proximity to a major metro.

Fort Mill and Tega Cay consistently rank among the fastest-growing cities in the Southeast. Rock Hill — the county's largest city — has emerged as a genuine mid-sized city with its own economic identity built on healthcare, higher education, and a booming downtown. And the waterfront communities of Lake Wylie and Clover attract affluent residents and entrepreneurs from both sides of the NC/SC border.

History & Economic Identity

York County's economic transformation is one of the most dramatic suburban growth stories in the American South. Historically an agricultural and textile county, York County's fortunes changed irrevocably when I-77 connected it directly to Charlotte — making the SC side of the state line an increasingly attractive destination for families seeking Charlotte incomes paired with South Carolina's lower taxes and cost of living.

Fort Mill exemplifies this transformation — once a small textile town, it is now home to LPL Financial, TechData (now TD SYNNEX), Shutterfly, and dozens of other corporate headquarters and regional offices that chose South Carolina over Charlotte's higher operating costs. The town's population has exploded, and its commercial corridors are among the most active in the entire Southeast.

Rock Hill has developed its own economic identity beyond the Charlotte shadow — anchored by Winthrop University (6,000+ students), Piedmont Medical Center, and a revitalized downtown Old Town district that has attracted restaurants, breweries, boutiques, and creative businesses. The city's proximity to Charlotte continues to fuel residential and commercial growth across the I-77 corridor.

Key Corridors & Communities

I-77 / Fort Mill Corridor
SC's Charlotte connection — corporate campuses, retail centers, and professional services lining the interstate corridor from the NC line to Rock Hill.
SC-160 / Fort Mill
Fort Mill's primary commercial artery — major retail, restaurants, medical offices, and professional services serving the county's fastest-growing residential communities.
Rock Hill / Cherry Road
Rock Hill's main commercial spine — retail, healthcare, auto dealers, and service businesses stretching from downtown to the I-77 interchange.
Old Town Rock Hill
A revitalized downtown arts and dining district — restaurants, breweries, boutique shops, and creative businesses anchoring Rock Hill's urban renaissance.
Lake Wylie / Clover
The western waterfront corridor — marinas, lakefront restaurants, vacation rentals, and real estate businesses serving the affluent Lake Wylie residential market.
Gold Hill Road / Tega Cay
The Tega Cay to Fort Mill commercial link — dense with retail, medical offices, childcare, and service businesses serving the county's young professional family demographic.
Carowinds Blvd Area
The entertainment and hospitality hub — Carowinds theme park, hotels, restaurants, and the Kingsley retail district at the NC/SC border.
York / US-321 Corridor
The county seat corridor connecting York to Rock Hill — small businesses, professional services, and the county government complex anchoring the historic inland communities.

Major Routes Across the County

🛣️
I-77 — Charlotte to Columbia
The economic lifeline of York County — connecting Rock Hill and Fort Mill directly to Charlotte (30 min) and Columbia (60 min), the primary driver of the county's explosive growth.
🛣️
US-21 — Rock Hill to Charlotte
The historic connector predating I-77 — still a commercial artery through the county linking Rock Hill, Fort Mill, and Pineville, NC with dense retail corridors.
🛣️
SC-160 / SC-49 — East-West Connector
The east-west spine linking Fort Mill to Lake Wylie and Clover — the primary cross-county commercial route serving the growing western communities.
