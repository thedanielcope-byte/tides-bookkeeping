About the Area

Spartanburg County Is
SC's Manufacturing
& Supply Chain Hub.

Spartanburg County is the manufacturing backbone of South Carolina's Upstate — and one of the most industrially significant counties in the entire Southeast. With BMW Manufacturing's sole US plant in the Greer/Duncan corridor, 50+ BMW supplier facilities, and a dense network of automotive, logistics, and industrial businesses, Spartanburg County generates more manufacturing export value per capita than almost anywhere in the country.

Beyond manufacturing, Spartanburg County has a vibrant downtown, two major universities (Wofford and Converse), a thriving healthcare system, and a growing small business community. The Hub City is becoming a destination — and that growth means more small business owners who need clean, organized financial records.

History & Economic Identity

Spartanburg County's economic story is one of transformation — from a 19th-century textile manufacturing powerhouse to a 21st-century global automotive and advanced manufacturing hub. The county's pivot began in 1992 when BMW selected a 1,150-acre site near the Greer/Duncan corridor for its first and only US manufacturing plant. That decision changed everything.

Today, BMW Manufacturing Co. produces the X3, X4, X5, X6, and X7 SUVs at the Spartanburg plant — the most exported US-built vehicle line, shipping to over 125 countries. The plant directly employs 11,000+ workers and has attracted more than 50 tier-1 and tier-2 suppliers to the I-85 corridor between Greenville and Spartanburg counties, collectively employing tens of thousands more.

Beyond BMW, Spartanburg County is home to Milliken & Company's global headquarters, Denny's Corporation's national headquarters, and a growing constellation of professional services, healthcare, and hospitality businesses anchored by Wofford College, Converse University, and Spartanburg Regional Healthcare System.

Key Corridors & Communities

Morgan Square / Downtown
Spartanburg's revitalized downtown core — restaurants, breweries, boutique shops, and professional offices anchored by the historic Morgan Square plaza.
I-85 / Duncan Industrial Corridor
The BMW supply chain spine — dense with automotive suppliers, logistics companies, industrial manufacturers, and distribution centers between Exits 63 and 72.
John B. White Blvd / Boiling Springs
Spartanburg's fastest-growing suburban commercial corridor — retail plazas, medical offices, restaurants, and professional services serving the booming residential communities.
East Main / Arts District
The arts and culture anchor of the city — Chapman Cultural Center, galleries, performance venues, and a growing cluster of creative businesses and studios.
Drayton Mills Corridor
Former textile mill redeveloped into mixed-use live/work/play — apartments, restaurants, and small businesses in a historic industrial setting.
I-26 / Woodruff Road
Southern Spartanburg County's commercial access route — connecting to Greenville County and anchoring distribution, logistics, and light industrial operations.
US-221 / Business I-85
The north-south spine through Spartanburg — connecting downtown to Boiling Springs and the northern communities, lined with retail and service businesses.
SC-9 / Inman-Landrum Corridor
Northern foothills corridor connecting Inman and Landrum — small manufacturers, equestrian estates, agricultural operations, and Blue Ridge-adjacent tourism businesses.

Major Routes Across the County
