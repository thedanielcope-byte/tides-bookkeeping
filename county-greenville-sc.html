History & Economic Identity

Greenville County's transformation from textile powerhouse to global manufacturing hub is one of the great economic reinvention stories in the American South. Once home to dozens of textile mills along the Reedy and Enoree rivers, the county pivoted dramatically in the 1990s and 2000s — anchored by major international investment that completely reshaped its economic identity.

BMW Manufacturing Co. opened its first US plant in Spartanburg in 1994 — just minutes from Greenville County's border in Greer — catalyzing an enormous supplier ecosystem that spans both counties. Michelin's North American headquarters sits directly in Greenville County, making it one of the most significant global tire manufacturing command centers in the world.

Today, Greenville County blends a thriving urban core in the City of Greenville with fast-growing suburban communities, rural townships, and one of the most active manufacturing corridors in the Southeast. That economic diversity creates a wide range of small business types — and a high demand for reliable bookkeeping.

Two major universities in the county — generating student-focused businesses, retail, housing, and professional services throughout the north and east sides of Greenville.

Key Corridors & Communities

Downtown Greenville
The county seat — a thriving urban core with restaurants, agencies, law firms, and tech startups anchored by Main Street and Falls Park.
Greer / I-85 Industrial Corridor
The BMW supply chain hub. Dense with manufacturers, logistics companies, and industrial services businesses along Hwy 29 and I-85.
Simpsonville / Five Forks
Fastest-growing suburb in the county. Strong retail, healthcare, and professional services along Woodruff Rd and Fairview Rd.
Mauldin / Butler Rd
South Greenville County's commercial zone — light industrial, auto services, and professional businesses along Butler and Bridges roads.
Wade Hampton / Taylors
The north-south spine running from downtown Greenville through Taylors toward the airport. Dense small business corridor.
Travelers Rest / Paris Mountain
Gateway to the Blue Ridge mountains — anchored by the Swamp Rabbit Trail. Home to outdoor recreation businesses, breweries, and boutique shops.
Fountain Inn
Southern gateway of the county along I-385. Growing manufacturing and distribution presence, plus a strong local downtown retail scene.
Gantt / Berea
Working-class communities on the west and south sides — trades, auto services, home improvement, and personal service businesses.

Major Routes Across the County

🛣️
I-85 — National Trade Corridor
Runs through Greer connecting Charlotte to Atlanta — the economic spine of the SC Upstate and the reason so many manufacturers chose Greenville County.
🛣️
I-385 — Greenville's Gateway
Connects downtown Greenville to I-85, passing through Mauldin, Simpsonville, and Fountain Inn. The primary commercial spine of southern Greenville County.
✈️
GSP International Airport (Greer)
Greenville-Spartanburg International Airport sits in Greer — making Greenville County a hub for travel-intensive businesses, logistics, and professionals who fly regularly.
