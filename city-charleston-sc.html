📍 About Charleston, SC

The City Behind the Business.

Charleston is one of America's oldest cities — founded in 1670, shaped by centuries of commerce, and now thriving as a modern business hub. Understanding its neighborhoods, economy, and landmarks is how we serve our clients better.

History & Economic Growth

Founded as Charles Town in 1670, Charleston was one of colonial America's most prosperous cities — a major port for rice, indigo, and cotton that built extraordinary wealth over several centuries. The city's historic built environment (the best-preserved in North America, some argue) is now one of its greatest economic assets, drawing millions of tourists annually and supporting a massive hospitality industry.

The modern economic transformation came in waves. Boeing's decision to build a 787 Dreamliner plant in North Charleston in 2009 brought aerospace manufacturing and its supply chain to the region. The Port of Charleston's continued expansion has made it one of the fastest-growing container ports in the country. And the rise of "Silicon Harbor" — a nickname for Charleston's tech cluster — has brought software, fintech, and startup companies that now employ thousands of knowledge workers across the region.

Today, Charleston balances being a world-class tourism destination with a serious, diversified business economy — making it one of the most dynamic small business markets in the South.

Downtown & Urban Neighborhoods

The Peninsula / Downtown
The historic heart of Charleston — Rainbow Row, King Street shopping, world-class restaurants, and boutique hotels packed onto one of America's most walkable urban cores.
King Street Corridor
Charleston's premier retail and dining street — from the antique district at the lower end through a dense strip of restaurants, boutiques, and galleries to the upper King bar district.
Daniel Island
Master-planned community northeast of the city — home to corporate offices, professional services, and a growing retail and restaurant scene serving a wealthy residential base.
Mount Pleasant
The largest and fastest-growing suburb — dense with retail corridors, restaurants, healthcare, and professional offices across the Cooper River from downtown.
North Charleston
Industrial and commercial backbone of the metro — home to Boeing, the port's inland logistics facilities, and a growing mixed-use redevelopment in the Navy Yard district.
West Ashley
Sprawling west-of-the-Ashley suburb with major retail corridors, medical offices, and a large base of service businesses serving Charleston's largest residential zone.

Outlying Communities

James Island
Residential island community with growing local businesses — popular with young professionals and families, with a tight-knit small business ecosystem.
Summerville
Rapidly growing suburban town 25 miles northwest — one of SC's fastest growing communities with booming retail and professional services.
Goose Creek / Hanahan
Berkeley County suburbs northwest of Charleston — military-adjacent communities with strong demand for service businesses and light industrial.
Johns Island / Kiawah
Coastal communities south of the city — anchored by world-class resort hospitality, outdoor tourism, and a growing base of local businesses.

I-26 (Primary Corridor)

Mark Clark Expressway (I-526)
The beltway connecting Mount Pleasant, North Charleston, and West Ashley — the primary cross-metro route for commuters and commercial traffic avoiding the peninsula.

US-17 (Savannah Hwy / Business)
Historic coastal highway running north-south through the metro — the spine of West Ashley's commercial corridor and the primary route toward Savannah and Myrtle Beach.

Coleman Blvd / US-17 (Mt. Pleasant)
Mount Pleasant's main commercial spine — running from the Ravenel Bridge through a dense corridor of restaurants, retail, and professional services.

Arthur Ravenel Jr. Bridge
The iconic cable-stayed bridge connecting the peninsula to Mount Pleasant — one of the longest cable-stayed bridges in the Western Hemisphere and a symbol of modern Charleston.
