About the Area

Lexington County Is
Columbia's Fastest-Growing
Suburban Market.

Lexington County is the third most populous county in South Carolina and one of the fastest-growing suburban markets in the Southeast. Directly west of Columbia across the Congaree River, the county has absorbed decades of population and business growth from the state capital — emerging as a major economic force in its own right with a business-friendly reputation, lower tax rates, and explosive residential development.

With Lexington Medical Center as the county's anchor employer, a booming retail and professional services sector along Augusta Highway and US-1, and the beautiful Lake Murray drawing recreation businesses and affluent residents to Chapin and Irmo — Lexington County has a uniquely diverse small business ecosystem that needs reliable, remote bookkeeping.

History & Economic Identity

Lexington County's economic story is inseparable from its neighbor across the river. As Columbia and Richland County grew into the state capital's political and institutional hub, Lexington County became its commercial and residential counterweight — offering lower taxes, newer infrastructure, and a business-friendly environment that drew families and entrepreneurs in droves from the 1980s onward.

Lexington Medical Center — a regional healthcare system employing 7,500+ — anchors the county's professional economy and generates an enormous ecosystem of medical practices, specialists, and healthcare vendors. Amazon's fulfillment center in Cayce, along with a string of manufacturing and distribution operations along I-26 and I-20, anchor a robust industrial sector on the county's eastern edge.

Lake Murray — a 50,000-acre reservoir created in 1930 — has become one of SC's premier recreational lakes, driving a year-round economy of marinas, restaurants, waterfront real estate, and outdoor recreation businesses in Chapin, Irmo, and the surrounding lakefront communities. Combined with explosive suburban growth in the Lexington town core, the county is one of SC's most dynamic small business markets.

Augusta Highway (US-1)
Lexington's main commercial artery — the spine of retail, medical offices, restaurants, and professional services running through the heart of the county seat.
Harbison Boulevard
One of SC's densest retail corridors — a massive commercial strip anchored by Columbiana Centre mall and stretching through both Lexington and Richland counties.
Cayce / State Street
Lexington County's industrial gateway — manufacturing, distribution, and a revitalizing downtown arts and restaurant scene along the Congaree River.
Lake Murray Blvd / Chapin

West Columbia / Meeting Street
A revitalizing commercial corridor across the Congaree from Columbia — restaurants, boutiques, and creative businesses driving a vibrant neighborhood commercial scene.
Edmund Highway / I-20 Corridor
Southern Lexington County's industrial and agricultural zone — manufacturing plants, agribusiness operations, and small businesses serving the rural communities.
Lexington Town Center
New development around the Lexington Medical Center campus — medical offices, retail plazas, restaurants, and professional services in the county's fastest-growing zone.
Batesburg-Leesville / US-178
Western Lexington County's rural commercial hub — manufacturing, agriculture, and small-town retail serving the communities near the Saluda and Aiken county lines.
