📍 About Spartanburg, SC

The City Behind the Business.

Spartanburg is a city of deep industrial heritage and surprising creative energy — nicknamed the "Hub City" for its original position at the center of seven railroad lines that once made it a commercial crossroads of the Southeast.

History & Economic Growth

Spartanburg was established in 1831 and spent much of its early history as a textile hub — with dozens of mills along the Pacolet, Tyger, and Lawson's Fork rivers driving the regional economy well into the 20th century. Like its Upstate neighbor Greenville, Spartanburg pivoted strategically as the textile industry declined, repositioning itself as a destination for international manufacturers.

The BMW connection was foundational. When BMW chose to build its first American plant in nearby Greer in 1994, it sat exactly on the border of Greenville and Spartanburg counties — bringing hundreds of global suppliers, many of whom set up operations across the Spartanburg County industrial corridor along I-85. Today, the county hosts manufacturers from Germany, Switzerland, Japan, France, and across the globe in a density unlike almost anywhere else in America.

Meanwhile, downtown Spartanburg's revitalization — anchored by the Chapman Cultural Center, Morgan Square, the Arts Center, and a growing restaurant scene — has created a vibrant small business environment that complements the industrial base.

Major Roads & Interstates

🛣️
I-85 (Primary Industrial Spine)
The main interstate through Spartanburg County — connecting the industrial corridor from the NC border through Gaffney and Duncan to the Greenville-Spartanburg metro. Hundreds of manufacturers line this corridor.
🛣️
I-26 (East-West)
Connects Spartanburg east to Columbia and west toward Asheville — the primary corridor for freight and commuters moving through the Upstate.
🛣️
US-29 / John B. White Blvd
Spartanburg's primary commercial arterial running northeast-southwest — dense with retail, restaurants, and service businesses serving the city's core residential areas.
🛣️
US-221 / Business I-85
Historic highway through downtown Spartanburg and the original business corridor — now home to a mix of established and emerging small businesses.
🛣️
SC-9 / New Cut Road
Key north-south connector linking Spartanburg to Boiling Springs and the northern suburbs — increasingly commercial with new retail and service development.
