📍 About South Carolina

A State Built for Business.

South Carolina is one of the fastest-growing states in the Southeast — with a diverse economy, business-friendly tax climate, and a distinct regional character that shapes how businesses operate from the Upstate to the Lowcountry.

⛰️
The Upstate
Greenville · Spartanburg · Anderson
The economic engine of SC — anchored by global manufacturers (BMW, Michelin, GE), a thriving Greenville downtown, and a rapidly growing professional services sector. Our anchor market.
🏛️
The Midlands
Columbia · Lexington · Aiken
Home to the state capital, Fort Jackson, the University of South Carolina, and a government-heavy economy supplemented by growing private sector development in the suburbs.
🌊
The Lowcountry
Charleston · Beaufort · Hilton Head
Tourism, hospitality, and real estate reign in SC's coastal region — historic Charleston, the resort communities of Hilton Head, and the port economy that drives the Southeast.
🌾
The Pee Dee & Grand Strand
Myrtle Beach · Florence · Conway
The Grand Strand is one of America's busiest tourist corridors — 60 miles of beach driving a massive hospitality economy, while the Pee Dee region is rooted in agriculture and manufacturing.

South Carolina's Economy & Business Climate

South Carolina is one of the most business-friendly states in America — with no inventory tax, a competitive corporate income tax rate, and a state government that has aggressively recruited global manufacturers for decades. The results speak for themselves: Boeing, Volvo, BMW, Michelin, Mercedes-Benz Vans, and dozens of others have made SC home.

SC was one of the original 13 colonies, ratifying the U.S. Constitution in 1788. It has a deep agricultural heritage — cotton, tobacco, and peaches shaped its rural economy for generations — but today's South Carolina is increasingly defined by manufacturing, tourism, healthcare, real estate, and a fast-growing technology sector.

🛳️
Port of Charleston — Southeast's Busiest Port
One of the top container ports on the East Coast — handling over $90B in cargo annually and supporting thousands of import/export businesses across SC.
✈️
Boeing South Carolina (North Charleston)
Boeing's 787 Dreamliner is assembled in North Charleston — one of the largest industrial employers in SC's Lowcountry and a magnet for aerospace suppliers statewide.
🏖️
Tourism — $24B+ Annual Industry
Myrtle Beach, Hilton Head, Charleston, and the SC mountains collectively drive one of the Southeast's most powerful tourism economies — with restaurants, hotels, short-term rentals, and retail all benefiting.
🪖
Military — Fort Jackson, Parris Island, JB Charleston
Three major military installations create a massive economic footprint — training thousands of recruits annually, supporting defense contractors, and anchoring the economies of Columbia, Beaufort, and North Charleston.
🏗️
Volvo Cars USA (Berkeley County)
Volvo's only US manufacturing plant, opened in 2018 — part of a long line of global auto manufacturers choosing SC for its workforce, infrastructure, and business climate.

Key Interstate & Highway Infrastructure

South Carolina's road network is one of its most underrated assets — connecting the Upstate to the coast and positioning the state as a key Southeast logistics hub.

I-85 — Upstate Spine
Connects Greenville–Spartanburg to Charlotte (NC) and Atlanta (GA) — the primary logistics artery for Upstate SC manufacturing.
I-26 — Columbia to Charleston
The primary corridor linking the Midlands to the coast — running from Spartanburg through Columbia to North Charleston and the port.
I-95 — East Coast Corridor
Runs the length of SC's eastern edge — connecting Myrtle Beach, Florence, and the Pee Dee to the national logistics network.
I-77 — York County Gateway
Connects Rock Hill and Fort Mill directly to Charlotte, NC — making York County one of SC's fastest-growing business corridors.
US-17 — Coastal Highway
The scenic coastal route linking Charleston, Myrtle Beach, and the Grand Strand — the commercial spine of SC's tourism economy.
I-20 — Augusta Connector
Connects Columbia to Augusta, GA — the primary route for Aiken County and the western Midlands commercial corridor.
