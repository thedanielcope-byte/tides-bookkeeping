History & Economic Identity

Founded in 1670, Charleston is one of America's oldest cities — and Charleston County has leveraged that history into a powerful economic identity built on tourism, culture, and international trade. The Port of Charleston, one of the ten busiest in the US, handles billions in container cargo annually and anchors a massive logistics and distribution ecosystem across the county.

Boeing South Carolina operates its only 787 Dreamliner widebody production facility in North Charleston — employing 7,000+ and generating an enormous aerospace supplier and service ecosystem throughout the county. Joint Base Charleston, home to over 22,000 military personnel and their families, drives a significant service economy across the county's northern communities.

Charleston's "Silicon Harbor" tech scene has emerged as one of the South's fastest-growing startup ecosystems — with software companies, fintech firms, and tech-enabled services businesses choosing the peninsula and surrounding areas for their headquarters. That growth, combined with the explosion of short-term rental operators and hospitality businesses, makes Charleston County one of the most bookkeeping-intensive markets in the Southeast.

Key Corridors & Communities

King Street / The Peninsula
Charleston's retail and restaurant spine — boutique shops, fine dining, craft cocktail bars, and art galleries driving a dense small business economy.
Mount Pleasant / Hwy 17
East Cooper's commercial artery — medical offices, retail centers, and professional services stretching from the Ravenel Bridge to Awendaw.
North Charleston / Rivers Ave
Industrial and commercial hub — Boeing, the airport, Tanger Outlets, and a dense corridor of automotive, logistics, and service businesses.
West Ashley / Savannah Hwy
West of the Ashley River — a mature suburban commercial corridor with restaurants, retail, healthcare, and professional services serving established neighborhoods.
Daniel Island
A master-planned community north of the city — corporate offices, professional services, and upscale dining anchored around Beresford Creek and the Volvo Car Stadium area.
Johns Island / Kiawah
Rapidly growing western corridor — construction contractors, agricultural operations, vacation rental managers, and boutique businesses serving resort communities.
Goose Creek / Hanahan
Industrial suburbs north of the city — defense contractors, manufacturing suppliers, and service businesses near Joint Base Charleston and the Goose Creek weapons station.
Folly Beach / Isle of Palms
Barrier island resort communities — vacation rental operators, beach restaurants, water sports businesses, and seasonal retail with complex revenue cycles.
