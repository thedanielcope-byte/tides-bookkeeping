📍 About Greenville, SC

The City Behind the Business.

Greenville is more than a zip code — it's one of the Southeast's most dynamic cities. Understanding its neighborhoods, roads, history, and economy is how we serve our clients better.

History & Economic Growth

Chartered in 1786 and incorporated in 1869, Greenville spent much of the 20th century as the "Textile Capital of the World" — home to dozens of mills lining the Reedy River. When the textile industry declined in the 1970s and '80s, Greenville made a deliberate pivot: recruiting global manufacturers, investing in a world-class downtown, and repositioning itself as a Southeast business hub.

The arrival of BMW's first US manufacturing plant in neighboring Greer in 1994 was the turning point — drawing hundreds of suppliers, engineers, and professional service firms to the region. The revival of Falls Park and the iconic Liberty Bridge in the early 2000s anchored a downtown renaissance that continues today. Greenville now ranks consistently among the best small cities in America to start and grow a business.

That growth means more small business owners than ever — and more small business owners who need clean books to grow with confidence.

Greenville's primary commercial corridor — dense with retail, dining, and offices from Haywood Mall to the I-385 interchange.

Historic residential-commercial mix north of downtown — locally-owned shops, galleries, and professional service businesses.

Central midtown spine — medical offices, service businesses, and dense small-business retail connecting downtown to the I-85 corridor.

Growing east-side neighborhoods with a mix of established businesses and new residential and commercial development.
