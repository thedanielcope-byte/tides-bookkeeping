About the Area

Richland County Is
SC's Political &
Professional Capital.

Richland County is the seat of South Carolina's state government and one of the most economically diverse counties in the Southeast. Home to the state capital — Columbia — the county blends government, higher education, healthcare, and a fast-growing private sector into a uniquely resilient economy.

With the University of South Carolina, Fort Jackson (the US Army's largest training post), Blue Cross Blue Shield of SC, and Prisma Health anchoring the employment base, Richland County generates a vast ecosystem of professional services, contractors, restaurants, and small businesses that need clean, reliable bookkeeping.

History & Economic Identity

Richland County's economy has always been shaped by its role as the seat of state power. Founded as the state capital in 1786, Columbia — and by extension Richland County — has grown alongside South Carolina's government apparatus for over two centuries. Today, that government presence anchors one of the most stable and diverse economies in the state.

Fort Jackson, the US Army's largest initial entry training center, pours tens of thousands of soldiers and families through Richland County annually — driving a massive service economy around housing, retail, automotive, and personal services. The University of South Carolina employs 7,500+ and generates an entire ecosystem of student-focused and professional businesses across the Five Points and Vista neighborhoods.

Beyond government and education, Richland County has developed strong healthcare, tech, and professional services sectors. Blue Cross Blue Shield of SC, Prisma Health, and Dominion Energy all have significant presences — creating a high density of corporate support businesses, consultants, and vendors who need clean, organized financials.

Key Corridors & Communities

Main Street / Downtown Columbia
The county seat's urban core — law firms, consulting agencies, restaurants, and tech startups in a rapidly revitalizing downtown district.
The Vista (Congaree Vista)
Columbia's arts and entertainment district — restaurants, bars, galleries, and event venues anchoring the revitalized former warehouse district.
Two Notch Road / Fort Jackson
The commercial spine leading to Fort Jackson — dense with auto dealers, fast food, retail chains, and service businesses serving the military community.
Garners Ferry / Hopkins
Southeastern Richland County's commercial corridor — a mix of healthcare facilities, local businesses, and contractors serving the southeastern communities.
Broad River / Irmo Corridor
Northwestern Richland's suburban shopping and service zone. Strong retail and professional office presence along Broad River Road and Bush River Road.
Forest Drive / Forest Acres
Upscale commercial corridor between downtown and Shandon — medical offices, law firms, insurance agencies, and specialty retailers.
I-77 / Blythewood Corridor
The rapidly growing northern growth axis — new retail, industrial parks, and residential development driving strong small business activity.
Harbison / Lake Murray Blvd
Western Richland County's retail hub — one of Columbia's densest shopping corridors, anchored by big-box retail and professional service plazas.

Major Routes Across the County

🛣️
I-26 — The Lowcountry Connection
Connects Columbia to Charleston on the southeast — a critical freight, tourism, and commerce corridor passing through the heart of Richland County.
🛣️
I-20 / Beltline Boulevard
Columbia's commercial ring road — circles the city and connects to I-26 and I-77, providing access to all major retail corridors and industrial areas.
